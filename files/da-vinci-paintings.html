      How much paintings Did Leonardo Da Vinci paint?
      Fifteen artworks are generally attributed either in whole or in large part to him. However, it is believed that he made many more, only for them to be lost over the years or remain unidentified. The authorship of several paintings traditionally attributed to Leonardo is disputed.

      Why Leonardo da Vinci paintings are famous?
      His natural genius crossed so many disciplines that he epitomized the term Renaissance man. Today he remains best known for his art, including two paintings that remain among the world's most famous and admired, Mona Lisa and The Last Supper. Art, da Vinci believed, was indisputably connected with science and nature.Dec 2, 2009

      Is Leonardo da Vinci the best painter?
      Leonardo has often been described as the archetype of the "Renaissance man", a man whose seemingly infinite curiosity was equalled only by his powers of invention. He is widely considered to be one of the greatest painters of all time and perhaps the most diversely talented person ever to have lived.

      Leonardo da Vinci
      Polymath

      Description

      Leonardo di ser Piero da Vinci, known as Leonardo da Vinci, was an Italian polymath of the Renaissance whose areas of interest included invention, drawing, painting, sculpture, architecture, science, music, mathematics, engineering, literature, anatomy, geology, astronomy, botany, paleontology, and cartography. Wikipedia
      Born: April 15, 1452, Anchiano, Italy
      Died: May 2, 1519, Chapel of Saint-Hubert, Amboise, France
      On view: Ambrosian Library, Louvre Museum, MORE
      Periods: High Renaissance, Early renaissance, Renaissance, Italian Renaissance, Florentine painting
      Books: The da Vinci Notebooks, A treatise on painting, MORE
      Series: Madonna of the Yarnwinder, Leda and the Swan
