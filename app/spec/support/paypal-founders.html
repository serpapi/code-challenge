    Who are the real founders of PayPal?
    Early history

    What would later become PayPal was originally established by Max Levchin, Peter Thiel, and Luke Nosek in December 1998 as Confinity, a company that developed security software for hand-held devices. Having had no success with that business model, however, it switched its focus to a digital wallet.

    Why are they called the PayPal Mafia?

    After PayPal was acquired by eBay, many of its co-founders and former employees left to open or invest in new tech companies together. Their tendency to keep their new businesses and investments in the PayPal family reminded many of the business practices of the Italian mafia. The group was later featured in a Fortune Magazine article with an accompanying photo of its most prominent members dressed like gangsters.
